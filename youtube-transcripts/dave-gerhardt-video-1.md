# Dave Gerhardt

## Video

Title: How to stand out when everyone is using AI (Copywriting Masterclass)

URL: https://youtu.be/VfmYv_QRUpM?si=1f1W1mXBCgcnH12a

## Transcript Highlights
1. Great copywriting is not about writing words; it is about influencing behavior and motivating action. Eddie discovered this when job ad performance depended on application rates rather than grammar or formatting.

2. Many successful content creators and thought leaders start by documenting what they are learning rather than trying to build an audience. Eddie's newsletter originated from personal notes and articles created to internalize copywriting concepts.

3. Content becomes more authentic and effective when it is created from genuine curiosity and experience rather than reverse-engineered for follower growth or vanity metrics.

4. AI-generated writing often tells readers what happened, while human writing allows readers to experience what happened through stories, emotions, dialogue, and implied meaning.

5. Inspired writing creates "holes" that allow readers to participate in the narrative by filling gaps with their own imagination and experiences.

6. Personal stories are powerful teaching tools because they transform abstract concepts into memorable lessons.

7. The process of writing is where insights are developed. Ideas often evolve dramatically through research, reflection, and revision rather than emerging fully formed from a prompt.

8. AI is most valuable for accelerating research, organizing information, and identifying patterns, but not for replacing creative thinking and original insight.

9. As AI raises the baseline quality of content, differentiation increasingly comes from personal experiences, unique perspectives, creativity, and human storytelling.

10. The future advantage for marketers is not producing more content faster; it is producing more human content that feels original, emotionally resonant, and experience-driven.

## Key Quotes
1. "Everybody can write now, but can you write?"

2. "The process of writing is where the work is done."

3. "The writing is good, but it's not inspired."

## Relevance To Research
This video provides valuable insight into LinkedIn organic content strategy because it highlights a growing challenge in B2B SaaS marketing: content commoditization through AI. Eddie argues that as AI makes competent writing accessible to everyone, differentiation shifts toward human experiences, original thinking, and emotionally engaging storytelling. This aligns with a broader trend among LinkedIn creators who are seeing stronger engagement from authentic, experience-based content compared to generic educational posts.

The discussion strongly emphasizes storytelling, thought leadership, personal branding, and audience connection. Eddie demonstrates how personal narratives create emotional resonance and make lessons more memorable than purely informational content. He also argues that content should emerge from genuine expertise and learning rather than being engineered solely for audience growth. This closely mirrors themes discussed by other LinkedIn growth experts such as creators who advocate for documenting experiences, sharing unique perspectives, and building authority through lived knowledge rather than content volume alone.

A particularly important insight for B2B SaaS content strategy is the distinction between AI-assisted workflows and AI-generated thinking. Eddie supports using AI for research, synthesis, and information gathering but insists that marketers must still go through the creative process themselves. This reinforces a recurring finding across leading content practitioners: successful organic content combines efficient systems with authentic human insight. The creators who stand out are not those publishing the most content, but those contributing original experiences, nuanced opinions, and stories that AI cannot replicate.

Overall, the video supports a key research theme emerging across LinkedIn growth, personal branding, and B2B SaaS marketing: as AI increases content supply, audience attention increasingly flows toward creators who communicate real experiences, demonstrate unique expertise, and create emotional connection through storytelling.
